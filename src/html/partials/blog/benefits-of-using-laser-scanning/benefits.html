Why Did the Construction Industry Shift to Laser Scanning?

Just like taking a photo is more accurate and less time-consuming than painting a picture, laser scanning is far more efficient than taking measurements by hand.

Architects and engineers used to visit construction sites to capture details, which was tedious and dangerous since some areas were under renovation. But in the 1990s, the more effective laser scanning technique was first used for site surveying. And since then, the construction industry has experienced a shift toward laser scanning.

With laser scanning, the need to be physically present for mapping at a construction site disappeared – an advantage that’s especially critical during the COVID-19 pandemic. It also made scanning inaccessible surfaces and complex geometry easier, and that is probably why construction giants use laser scanning in their work.

What Tasks Does Laser Scanning Solve in Construction?

As you already know, laser scanning is used for mapping a design site and collecting accurate information about a building project in 2-3 days without involving multiple people. Other tasks laser scanning can solve in the construction industry are:

  • Monitoring the construction progress. Laser scanning aids in checking building progress and estimating its accuracy. Additionally, this technology is beneficial for quality control and verifying contract fulfillment.
  • Mapping site conditions in renovation. With 3D laser scanning, the construction team evaluates site conditions in renovation projects quickly. It also helps to find errors in construction plans and make these plans more accurate. 3D laser scanning helps to monitor the accuracy of architectural design and create site documentation.
  • Determining design problems. As a construction project progresses, design issues may arise in the form of new drawings or changes to a building plan. Laser scanning can help construction companies avoid these issues. Also, this technology is used to provide precise deliverables as sections, floor plans, elevations, and more.

Indeed, laser scanning can help solve specific construction problems, but is it really that useful for the entire industry? Short answer: yes!

How the Construction Industry Benefits from Laser Scanning

Laser scanning helps construction industry players cut costs, streamline the building process, and properly evaluate risks. Along with these benefits, laser scanning offers:

  • Improved quality. Using the laser scanning technique gives engineering teams the most accurate information about building sites. Laser scanning captures internal and external details with an accuracy of up to 0.04 inches. This allows creating precise architectural projects without having to redo them later.
  • Reduced costs. Speaking of accuracy, precise measurement and swift data collection are the factors that reduce costs. According to BuilderSpace, laser scanning saves up to 10% on building projects. Other than that, by using this technology in your building project, you will not have the need to send a construction team on the site.
  • Streamlined collaboration. Laser scanning helps to improve collaboration and coordination. When teams receive a 3D virtual site overview, they can simultaneously work on it, regardless of the location. This helps construction companies improve their teams’ collaboration, communication, and decision-making.
  • Improved design and planning. Laser scanning allows construction teams to analyze clashes between site conditions and newly added design elements. Plus, it improves the planning process, ensures exact site measurements and the materials the construction company should use.

    Some building projects require remodeling of the existing spaces and architecture. In this case, 3D laser scanning is used to create, refine, and realize design ideas. This provides precise deliverables as sections, floor plans, elevations, and more.

  • Increased speed. No tape measure can compete with laser scanning: one scan takes up to two minutes. Besides, laser scanning ensures quick access to site details without the need to visit a building area since everyone can see all the captured data in the office.
  • Enhanced safety measures. With laser scanning, construction companies can improve safety measures during the surveying process. For instance, portable laser scanners are effective in recording measurements from a 500-meter distance. That keeps the construction team safe if they work on potentially hazardous areas.
  • Enhanced accuracy and flexibility. Manual labor is tedious and prone to errors, unlike automatic laser scanning. The technology helps to minimize the number of mistakes in the construction project. On top of that, laser scanning reduces the time spent on building sites, making working processes more flexible.
