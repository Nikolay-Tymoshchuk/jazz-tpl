Best BIM Tools in 2021

In the previous section, we covered a range of BIM software categories with examples, which, together, represent a classic BIM software list. This means that choosing a set of BIM tools to suit your project needs might be a challenging task, given that most of the popular solutions (Navisworks, for example) for building information modeling belong to several categories at the same time.

Luckily, there’s a way to simplify the selection process. Every BIM stack is built around one technology with core functionalities, which is, in most cases, 3D modeling. So, the key is to first focus on finding your “core” technology and then, based on your project needs, complement it with other tools.

The software that will lie at the heart of your processes should be a reliable solution that aligns with your project requirements, budgetary constraints, level of BIM adoption, and other factors. Below are some best-on-the-market examples of the top BIM software for 2021.

  • Revit

    Perhaps, no BIM software comparison can be made without mentioning Revit. Created by Autodesk, it’s a catch-all solution that covers the full spectrum of building construction activities, including planning, designing, implementation, and even further maintenance.

    According to many architects, “the capabilities of this software are exceptional,” and we cannot help but agree with this statement. But what makes Revit “exceptional”? Here are some examples:

    • It facilitates collaboration — one of the central BIM concepts. It incorporates all AEC-relevant disciplines, allowing everyone involved in the building process, from architects and structural engineers to electricians and plumbers, to communicate with each other and coordinate their processes more efficiently.
    • It creates models with interdependent components. This means that when you move, say, a wall, all the neighboring elements will update automatically.
    • Thanks to 4D simulation capabilities, Revit allows planning and tracking various stages of a building’s entire life cycle.
    • Due to Revit’s generative design functionalities, you can come up with numerous design alternatives of any LOD (Level of Detail) even when your project is in full swing.
    • It’s interoperable. You can easily extend Revit’s in-built functionalities with third-party plugins and add-ons.

  • Archicad

    Archicad is another popular 3D modeling software. Created for architects and interior architects in Hungary in 1984, it’s considered by some AEC professionals to be the first BIM software for architects. But the fascinating fact about Archicad is that despite being almost four decades old, it remains an industry leader — thanks to regular updates, such as:

    • BIMcloud — a collaboration environment for various project stakeholders, including an MEP (mechanical, electrical, and plumbing) engineer. Due to the inclusion of BIMcloud in 2020, Archicad is now considered a direct competitor to Revit.
    • EcoDesigner STAR — a sustainable design solution, which allows Archicad users to perform energy-evaluation analysis of their projects based on real-time weather data and BIM geometry analysis. With EcoDesigner STAR, architects can design energy-efficient buildings without the assistance of sustainability consultants.
    • BIMx — a visualization tool for mobile. It allows users to present and interact with 3D Archicad models and 2D documentation in a more intuitive format.

    Archicad is suitable for projects of varying size and complexity and is available for researchers, educators, and architecture students in its full functionality for free.

  • ALLPLAN

    ALLPLAN is Nemetschek’s BIM software for object-oriented 3D design. Created for both architects and designers, it’s efficient in handling various construction challenges, including creation of the initial draft, finalization of working drawings, as well as cost optimization in the context of personnel shortage and increasing project complexity. It has the following features:

    • Multiple design options, including precise 2D and 3D modeling.
    • Bridge construction capabilities.
    • Centralizes all tasks, documents, and information related to the project.
    • Collaboration between everyone involved in the project.
    • Supports different data formats, enabling seamless data exchange.

    On top of that, ALLPLAN is known for its easy-to-use interface, making it an excellent option for everyone who’s just testing the waters ith BIM software.

  • Tekla Structures

    Tekla Structures enables construction industry professionals to create and manage highly accurate 3D models complemented with comprehensive and detailed construction information. The best part is that you shouldn’t create all design elements from scratch—you can streamline the design process by using ready-made items designed and uploaded by the members of Tekla’s construction community.

    It’s also a perfect solution for collaboration with capabilities to merge and share 3D models, as well as to detect clashes in design. On top of that, being compatible with a variety of devices and screens, Tekla can be used at the construction site to collaborate on the live projects, enabling site engineers to plan daily schedules and even make onsite changes during the project execution.

  • MicroStation—Bentley Systems

    Initiated as a 2D CAD software, MicroStation has evolved into a powerful parametric 3D modeling solution with intelligent drafting capabilities. It enables AEC professionals to collaborate on construction projects of varying size and complexity. MicroStation is suitable for hydraulic, hydrologic, transportation, and structural engineering. Its main features include:

    • 2D and 3D design modeling capabilities
    • Allows to generate plans for your production process, as well as to animate sequence of execution steps, create relations between objects for dynamic updating.
    • Enables multiple users to collaborate on one project.
    • Clash detection.

    In addition, Microstation is ideal for beginners. With its in-app learning capabilities, video tutorials, and truly helpful support, you can quickly get a handle on this software without doing additional research.

  • VectorWorks

    Created in 1985 in the US, VectorWorks includes both BIM and CAD tools. As an end-to-end solution that can help you improve your entire workflow, VectorWorks can support you in any process and project stage, from conceptual design to coordinated BIM models, without the need for third-party software.

    Moreover, it allows you to run “what if” scenarios early in the project and integrates well with third-party tools, including SketchUp and Revit. On top of that, VectorWorks has a 30-day free trial and a free version for architecture researchers, teachers, and students.

  • Navisworks

    Another Autodesk solution, Navisworks is a reliable project review tool with simulation, animation, and data aggregation capabilities.

    Due to its clash detection, visualization, and dynamic updating functionalities, Navisworks allows AEC professionals to predict project outcomes and to prevent risks before the construction begins. On top of that, it supports 4D and 5D simulations, which allow planning and controlling costs and implementation time frames in advance.
