Which BIM Software to Choose

When it comes to choosing the best BIM software for your project, don’t focus on finding a product that incorporates all BIM features — you won’t find it on the market. So, your BIM coordinator should be prepared to look for several solutions that complement each other. To set up a perfect BIM workflow, you’ll need the following categories of tools:

  • Tools to create a common data environment (CDE) for storing all project data. If you are just testing the waters with BIM, creating a local disk on a computer and sharing it (the disk) with all the project’s participants will suffice. However, be prepared to move to the cloud. Luckily, there’s a wide variety of suitable offerings on the market, including BIM 360, Trimble Connect, Revizto, ALLPLAN Bimplus, Tekla BIMsight, and so on.
  • Document creation software. Notes, summaries, presentations, and descriptions are integral parts of any construction project. So don’t neglect tools for document creation, such as G-Suite or Microsoft 365.
  • Communication tools. Communication and collaboration are the cornerstones of BIM ideology. So, make sure your team knows how to use popular solutions like Microsoft Teams, Microsoft Outlook, or Slack.
  • BIM coordination software. The BIM methodology entails collaboration between various project participants regardless of their disciplines. To facilitate multidisciplinary collaboration, you can use Navisworks Manage, Solibri Model Checker, Trimble, Autodesk Construction Cloud, Tekla BIMsight, BIM Track, and so on.
  • Issue management tools. Clashes — when two elements of your design take up the same space — are not uncommon in architecture. With reliable clash detection and collision management software in place, such as Navisworks or BIM Track, you can prevent these in the pre-construction stages.
  • BIM 4D tools One of the most valuable BIM software features is the simulation of the workflow on the construction site. You can generate these simulations using Synchro, Navisworks, etc.
  • 3D modeling software. 3D models are at the heart of BIM methodology and today’s construction industry in general. Revit, Archicad, and Vectorworks, are just a few examples. When classic 3D modeling tools don’t suffice, consider “unlimited” geometric tools, such as Dynamo, Grasshopper, etc.
  • AR and VR BIM solutions. Though AR and VR are not a must for BIM, tools like Fuzor, Revizto, or Iris VR can considerably improve your decision-making.
  • Supplementary tools. BIM is not just 2D and 3D modeling, project planning, and execution management. No BIM project is complete without document creation software, such as G-Suite or MMicrosoft 365, and communication tools, such as Microsoft Teams, Microsoft Outlook, or Slack..

As you can see, most of these software categories, such as communication or document creation tools, are not meant exclusively for architectural design. But together, they will help you to create an ideal BIM workflow, allowing you to plan, design, and construct buildings and infrastructure more efficiently.
